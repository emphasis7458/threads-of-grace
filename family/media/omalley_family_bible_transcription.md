**Letter content**:
> Dear Mrs. O'Malley:
>
> You don't know me but I am a little bit kin to you through the SEGRAVES family. I am enclosing a sheet showing how I am descended from Sherrod SEGRAVES who lived in Dyer County and died in 1893. I talked with Mrs. A.H. (Martha) BLEDSOE about the SEGRAVES family and she said that perhaps you would know more than she did about the family. (She said you were the one in the family with a lot of sense.)
>
> If I understood Mrs. BLEDSOE correctly, your mother was a COTTON. Were your grandparents named Dan COTTON and Annie May SEGRAVES? From the census records I learned that Annie May SEGRAVES was born about 1870 and she was the daughter of George W. SEGRAVES and Eliza Harriet ENOCHS. On 15 Nov 1888 in Dyer County, Annie May married Dan COTTON. And that is all the information that I have on this family. Who were their children? How are you related to them? Was George W. SEGRAVES' full name George Washington SEGRAVES?
>
> I am not any relation to the Frank WALKER who married Rosa SEGRAVES (the sister of Mrs. BLEDSOE). I am descended from the SEGRAVES on my mother's side. See the enclosed sheet and I hope you understand exactly how I am related to your family. I am trying to gather all the information I can on the SEGRAVES. Someday I hope to publish it. Any information you can give me will be very helpful. I hope you have a merry Christmas!
>
> Sincerely yours,
> Johnny D. Walker

### Document 11: Newspaper Obituary - Dan Cotton (Page 19)

**Headline**: DAN COTTON DIES EARLY THURSDAY

**Date notation**: 2-24-31 [February 24, 1931]

**Subheadline**: Beloved Citizen Passes Away At Dyersburg Following Illness Of Ten Days

> Dan Cotton, Sr., beloved citizen of Dyersburg, public official and pioneer resident of the county, died at his home here on Miller street at two o'clock Thursday morning following an illness of ten days. He was seventy-one years of age on October 8, 1930.
>
> Funeral services will be held at the Cumberland Presbyterian church of which he was an ardent and active member here at two o'clock Friday afternoon, conducted by the Rev. H. L. Hinch, pastor, and Rev. George P. Scott. Burial will be at Dyersburg.
>
> His parents were pioneer residents of West Tennessee and Mr. Cotton was born in Dyersburg and had resided in Dyer county all of his life, except while managing his father's plantation in Missouri for a short while.

**Public Official**:
> Mr. Cotton's mother was a member of the Clark family, who built Dyer county's first log courthouse on the banks of the Forked Deer river here and who also furnished the county's first sheriff.
>
> Mr. Cotton himself had a long and enviable career as a public official. He was for several terms trustee of the county and was for two years chairman of Dyer county court, his term expiring this year. He was a member of the court from the fourth district at the time of his death.
>
> Prior to his entering official life, Mr. Cotton was in the hardware business here and later was a real estate dealer. He always enjoyed the respect and highest esteem of his fellow citizens, business associates and brother officials and more than once was appointed to the position of leader among them.

**Widely Known**:
> Due to his many years in public life, Mr. Cotton was one of the best and most widely known men in the county and his passing Thursday was learned with sincere regret throughout Dyer county and in West Tennessee, where he also was prominently known.
